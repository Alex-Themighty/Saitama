Theory & Formulas

A binomial distribution describes the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with the same probability of success.

Example: Finding 4 people with blue eyes in 10 trials, the probability of finding blue eyes people are 0.3 every time

  1. Probability Mass Function (PMF): $P(X=x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x (1-p)^{n-x}$
  2. Expected Value (Mean): $E(X) = \mu = np$
  3. Variance: $Var(X) = \sigma^2 = np(1-p)$
  4. Standard Deviation: $sd(X) = \sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)}$
  5. Conditional Probability: $P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$
  6. Complementary Rule: $P(X \ge k) = 1 - P(X < k)$
  7. Cumulative Probability: $P(X \le x) = \sum_{k=0}^{x} \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}$
