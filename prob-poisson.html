Theory & Formulas

A Poisson distribution describes the probability of a given number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space, given a constant average rate of occurrence.

Example: Finding the probability of receiving exactly 5 emails in an hour, given that you receive an average of 3 emails per hour.

  1. Probability Mass Function (PMF): $P(X=x) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^x}{x!}$
    Here, $\lambda$ (lambda) is the average rate of occurrence (the expected number of successes) in your given interval. $e$ is Euler's number ($\approx 2.71828$), and $x$ is the target number of successes.
  2. Expected Value (Mean): $E(X) = \mu = \lambda$
  3. Variance: $Var(X) = \sigma^2 = \lambda$
  4. Standard Deviation: $sd(X) = \sigma = \sqrt{\lambda}$
  5. Conditional Probability: $P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$
  6. Complementary Rule: $P(X \ge k) = 1 - P(X < k)$
  7. Cumulative Probability: $P(X \le x) = \sum_{k=0}^{x} \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^k}{k!}$
