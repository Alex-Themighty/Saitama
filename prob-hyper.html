Theory & Formulas

The Hypergeometric distribution models the probability of a specific number of successes when you draw a sample from a finite population without replacement.

Example: Drawing 5 cards from a standard 52-card deck (without putting them back) and wanting to find the probability of getting exactly 2 Hearts.

  1. The Combination Rule (The Building Block): $\binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$

    Explanation: A combination tells us how many different ways we can choose a group of $r$ items from a larger pool of $n$ items, when the order we pick them doesn't matter. We read it as "$n$ choose $r$".

  2. Probability Mass Function (PMF): $P(X=x) = \frac{\binom{K}{x} \binom{N-K}{n-x}}{\binom{N}{n}}$

    $N$: total population size
    $n$: sample size
    $K$: total success
    $x$: desire success

  3. Expected Value (Mean): $E(X) = n \cdot \frac{K}{N}$
  4. Variance: $Var(X) = n \cdot \frac{K}{N} \cdot \left(\frac{N-K}{N}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{N-n}{N-1}\right)$

    *Note: The last fraction $\frac{N-n}{N-1}$ is known as the "finite population correction factor".

  5. Standard Deviation: $sd(X) = \sqrt{Var(X)}$
