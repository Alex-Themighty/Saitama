Theory & Formulas

The Geometric distribution models the number of trials needed to achieve the first success in a series of independent Bernoulli trials (where each trial has only two outcomes: success or failure).

Example: Rolling a standard die repeatedly until you finally roll a 6, or flipping a coin until you finally land on Heads.

  1. Probability Mass Function (PMF): $P(X=x) = (1-p)^{x-1}p$

    Here, $p$ is the probability of a success on any single trial.
    $x$ is the specific trial number on which the first success occurs. This means there are $(x-1)$ failures before the first success happens.

  2. Expected Value (Mean): $E(X) = \mu = \frac{1}{p}$
  3. Variance: $Var(X) = \sigma^2 = \frac{1-p}{p^2}$
  4. Standard Deviation: $sd(X) = \sigma = \frac{\sqrt{1-p}}{p}$
  5. Cumulative Probability: $P(X \le x) = 1 - (1-p)^x$
  6. Complementary Rule (Survival): $P(X > x) = (1-p)^x$
  7. The Memoryless Property: $P(X > x + k \mid X > x) = P(X > k)$

    High School Explanation: Imagine you are flipping a coin trying to get Heads. If you just flipped 5 Tails in a row (you've already "survived" $x$ trials without success), the coin doesn't "remember" those failures. Your probability of needing 3 more flips to finally get a Heads ($k$ trials) is exactly the same as if you just picked up the coin and started fresh. Past failures don't change future odds!
